Audio samples from "Semi-supervised Generative Modeling for Controllable End-to-end Text-to-Speech and Voice Conversion"

Authors: Hai Zhu, Huayi Zhan, Hong Cheng, Ying Wu

Abstract:
Speech synthesis (text to speech, TTS) and conversion (voice conversion, VC) are important speech tasks and have made great progress due to the development of deep learning. This paper proposes a controllable end-to-end TTS-VC approach by constructing a space of disentangled representations of linguistic information, speaker identity information, and latent attribute information. By providing partial supervision, we can reliably discover and control important but rarely labeled attribute (such as emotion) in the speech. We can demonstrate that as little as 1% (about 2 minutes) of supervision can be sufficient to control emotional styles. Experimental results show that our proposed model achieves extraordinary performance and consistently outperforms both TTS and VC baseline frameworks.

We use the Mandarin corpus from Emotional Speech Dataset (ESD) for training our models. This dataset is spoken by 10 native Mandarin speakers and consists of 350 parallel utterances with an average duration of 2.9 seconds. The audio format is 16-bit PCM with a sample rate of 16 kHz. We resample the sample rate to 22 kHz. The text format is the character, which we converted into phonemes through pypinyin toolkit, and we carefully proofread the conversion results manually. The data set consists of 5 male and 5 female speakers. For each speaker, the corpus consists of five emotions as follows: happy, sad, neutral, angry, and surprise. We split the corpus for a speaker into a training set (300 samples), a validation set (30 samples), and a test set (20 samples).
